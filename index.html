📚 Yoga Fundamentals

What is Yoga?

Yoga is a multifaceted, experiential science — not merely physical exercise. Patanjali defines it as "Chitta Vritti Nirodha" — the stilling of the fluctuations of the mind.

🔬

Experiential Science

Yoga is both subjective and objective — a living science proven through inner experience, not just intellectual study. It integrates body, breath, mind, and consciousness.

🌐

Multifaceted System

Yoga encompasses dynamic practices (Asana, Pranayama), meditative practices (Dharana, Dhyana, Samadhi), ethical codes (Yama, Niyama), and inward withdrawal (Pratyahara).

🎯

Universal Objective

The ultimate aim is Samadhi — a state of complete union and self-realisation. Practically, it promotes physical health, mental clarity, emotional balance, and spiritual growth.

Dynamic & Meditative Practices

Yoga offers a complete spectrum — from active, energising dynamic practices to deeply inward meditative techniques. Each serves a unique purpose in holistic wellness.

Asana

Physical postures for strength, flexibility & balance

💨

🧘

Concentration & meditation for mental stillness

✨

Samadhi

Supreme absorption — union of self with the whole

🌙 Yoga Nidra

Origin, Introduction & Authenticity

Yoga Nidra — "Yogic Sleep" — is an ancient meditative practice with roots in the Tantric tradition. Systematised by Swami Satyananda Saraswati of the Bihar School of Yoga, it is the authoritative modern approach.

📜

Ancient Origin

Yoga Nidra has its roots in the Nyasa practices of Tantra Shastra and the Mandukya Upanishad's description of the four states of consciousness. Swami Satyananda Saraswati systematised it into a scientific technique in the 20th century at the Bihar School of Yoga.

🧠

What Is Yoga Nidra?

A state of conscious sleep — the practitioner lies in Shavasana while the mind is guided through successive stages: body rotation, breath awareness, pairs of opposite sensations, visualisation, and Sankalpa (intention). The result: profound relaxation at physical, mental, emotional and spiritual levels.

🎖 Authentic Lineage

The Bihar School of Yoga, founded by Swami Satyananda Saraswati, is the authoritative source. Practised globally, Yoga Nidra is now recognised by modern neuroscience for inducing theta brainwave states linked to deep healing, creativity, and subconscious reprogramming.

"Yoga Nidra is the art of conscious relaxation where healing begins from within. A few minutes equals hours of restful sleep."

🪷 Ashtang Yoga

Eight Limbs of Ashtang Yoga

Patanjali's Yoga Sutras describe the eightfold path (Ashtanga) as the complete system for attaining liberation. Each limb builds upon the previous, culminating in Samadhi.

1

Yama

Ethical Restraints

Non-violence (Ahimsa), Truthfulness, Non-stealing, Celibacy, Non-possessiveness. The moral foundation of Yoga practice.

2

Niyama

Personal Observances

Purity, Contentment, Discipline, Self-study, Surrender to the Divine. Internal discipline for inner growth.

3

Asana

Physical Postures

Stable, comfortable postures that prepare the body for meditation. Promotes strength, flexibility, and body awareness.

4

Pranayama

Breath Control

Regulation of life-force (Prana) through breath. Energises, calms and prepares the mind for deeper inward practices.

5

Pratyahara ⭐

Withdrawal of Senses — Yoga Nidra Lives Here

The inward withdrawal of the senses from external objects. Yoga Nidra is a profound practice of Pratyahara — the gateway between the outer (dynamic) and inner (meditative) limbs of Yoga. Through conscious relaxation, awareness is drawn inward, making it one of the most powerful pratyahara tools available.

6

Dharana

Concentration

Single-pointed focus on an object, mantra, or visualisation. Trains the mind to remain steady and undistracted.

7

Dhyana

Meditation

Uninterrupted flow of awareness towards the object of focus. Deep meditation that transcends thought and brings inner peace.

8

Samadhi

Union & Liberation

The highest state — complete absorption of the individual self into pure consciousness. The ultimate goal of Ashtang Yoga.

Root-Cause Approach

Unlike symptomatic treatment, IAYT identifies and addresses the underlying physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual causes of illness — creating lasting transformation.
