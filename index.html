🧘 Foundations of Yoga

Understanding Yoga

Yoga is an experiential science, not merely a form of physical exercise. According to Maharshi Patanjali: "Yogaḥ Chitta Vritti Nirodhaḥ" — Yoga is the stilling of the fluctuations of the mind, leading to harmony of body, mind, and consciousness.
🔬

Experiential Science

Yoga is a practical science experienced through regular practice, integrating body, breath, mind, and consciousness to promote inner awareness rather than merely intellectual understanding.

🌿

Multifaceted Practice

Yoga includes ethical values, physical postures, breathing techniques, meditation, deep relaxation, and self-awareness, creating a balanced lifestyle for physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual wellbeing.

🎯

Universal Objective

The ultimate purpose of Yoga is inner peace and self-realisation. Its regular practice enhances physical health, emotional stability, mental clarity, and spiritual growth for people of all ages.

🌙 Yoga Nidra

Origin, Introduction & Authenticity

Yoga Nidra, often called "Yogic Sleep", is an ancient meditative practice rooted in the Tantric tradition. It was systematically presented by Swami Satyananda Saraswati of the Bihar School of Yoga, making it one of the most authentic and scientifically structured relaxation practices.

📜

Ancient Origin

Yoga Nidra traces its roots to the Nyasa practices of Tantra and the Mandukya Upanishad's description of the four states of consciousness. Swami Satyananda Saraswati later systematised it into a structured scientific practice at the Bihar School of Yoga.

🧠

What is Yoga Nidra?

Yoga Nidra is a state of conscious relaxation where the body rests deeply while awareness remains awake. Through body rotation, breath awareness, visualisation and Sankalpa, practitioners experience profound physical, mental, emotional and spiritual relaxation.

🎖 Authentic Lineage

Yoga Nidra as taught by the Bihar School of Yoga preserves the authentic traditional method. Modern neuroscience also recognises its role in deep relaxation, emotional regulation, creativity, stress reduction and subconscious transformation.

"Yoga Nidra is the art of conscious relaxation where healing begins from within. A few minutes of Yoga Nidra may provide the restorative effects of several hours of restful sleep."

📖 Mind-Body Philosophy

Yoga Vasistha: Adhi & Vyadhi

The ancient text Yoga Vasistha reveals how psychological stress transforms into physical illness. It provides a profound understanding of holistic health long before modern psychosomatic medicine.

🧠
The Root Cause

Adhi (Primary Mental Distress)

Adhi refers to disorders originating in the mind. Uncontrolled desires, emotional turbulence, anxiety, and ignorance (Ajnana) disrupt the smooth flow of Prana (vital energy) in the subtle body.

Key Manifestations:

Chronic mental stress, unchecked desires, emotional instability, and agitated thought patterns.

🩺
The Physical Result

Vyadhi (Physical Disease)

Vyadhi represents physical ailment. When the flow of Prana is disturbed by Adhi, it impairs digestion (Agni) and metabolism, eventually manifesting as physical diseases in the body.

Key Manifestations:

Digestive imbalances, psychosomatic disorders, physiological fatigue, and bodily pain.

⚡ Psychosomatic Mechanism

The Process from Mind to Body

According to Sage Vasistha, physical health is restored when mental equilibrium is regained through conscious practice and self-realization.

01

Mental Disturbance

Unfulfilled desires and continuous mental agitation generate persistent inner stress (Adhi).

02

Pranic Imbalance

Agitated thoughts disrupt the natural flow of life energy (Prana) throughout the channels (Nadis).

03

Metabolic Disruption

Erratic Prana affects digestive fire (Agni), resulting in poor digestion and toxification.

04

Physical Illness

Toxins accumulate and manifest as physical diseases and organic ailments (Vyadhi).

🪷 Ashtang Yoga

Eight Limbs of Ashtang Yoga

Patanjali's Yoga Sutras describe the eightfold path (Ashtanga) as the complete system for attaining liberation. Each limb builds upon the previous, culminating in Samadhi.

1

Yama

Ethical Restraints

Non-violence (Ahimsa), Truthfulness, Non-stealing, Celibacy, Non-possessiveness. The moral foundation of Yoga practice.

2

Niyama

Personal Observances

Purity, Contentment, Discipline, Self-study, Surrender to the Divine. Internal discipline for inner growth.

3

Asana

Physical Postures

Stable, comfortable postures that prepare the body for meditation. Promotes strength, flexibility, and body awareness.

4

Pranayama

Breath Control

Regulation of life-force (Prana) through breath. Energises, calms and prepares the mind for deeper inward practices.

5

Pratyahara ★

Withdrawal of Senses

The inward withdrawal of the senses. Yoga Nidra is a profound practice of Pratyahara — the gateway between outer and inner limbs. Through conscious relaxation, awareness is drawn deep inward.

6

Dharana

Concentration

Single-pointed focus on an object, mantra, or visualisation. Trains the mind to remain steady and undistracted.

7

Dhyana

Meditation

Uninterrupted flow of awareness towards the object of focus. Deep meditation that transcends thought and brings inner peace.

8

Samadhi

Union & Liberation

The highest state — complete absorption of the individual self into pure consciousness. The ultimate goal of Ashtang Yoga.
